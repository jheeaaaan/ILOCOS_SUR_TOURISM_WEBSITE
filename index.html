Welcome

A Journey Through Time & Beauty

Ilocos Sur, located on the northwestern coast of Luzon, is a province where the Philippines' rich colonial heritage comes alive. Its capital, Vigan City, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, one of the few surviving examples of a planned Spanish colonial town in Asia.

Beyond cobblestone streets and ancestral houses, Ilocos Sur offers pristine beaches, dramatic waterfalls, highland adventures, world-class Ilocano cuisine, and festivals that celebrate centuries of culture and tradition.

Taste of Ilocos

Ilocano Food & Delicacies

Bold, savory, unforgettable. Ilocano cuisine is renowned across the Philippines.

🥩

Bagnet

The Ilocano version of crispy lechon, double-fried pork belly perfection

🥟

Empanada

Deep-fried orange pastry filled with egg, longganisa, and green papaya

🍖

Longganisa

Vigan's famous garlicky-sour sausage, best served for breakfast

🥘

Pinakbet

The original Ilocano vegetable stew that is earthy, hearty, and nourishing
